00:05 You: Thanks for making time. Before I show you anything, I'd love to understand how your team runs deals today.
00:14 Prospect: Sure. We're a 40-person sales org, mostly mid-market SaaS. I run the AE team.
00:24 You: Got it. What does the handoff look like after a deal closes?
00:33 Prospect: Honestly, it's messy. AEs close, then implementation picks it up from notes in the CRM, which are usually incomplete.
00:46 You: When was the last time an incomplete handoff actually caused a problem?
00:55 Prospect: Two weeks ago. We onboarded a customer and the implementation team didn't know the customer had asked for SSO before signature. Kickoff slipped a week.
01:10 You: Ouch. How often does something like that happen?
01:18 Prospect: I'd say one in three deals has some kind of context loss. Most are small, but the bad ones cost us renewal goodwill.
01:32 You: Can you put a number on what a slipped kickoff costs you?
01:41 Prospect: Implementation reckons each slipped week is roughly two grand in services time, plus the CSM scramble. Multiply by maybe four deals a month.
01:58 You: Have you tried fixing it with process or tooling already?
02:07 Prospect: We added a mandatory handoff form in Salesforce last quarter. AEs hate it, half fill it in after the fact from memory.
02:21 You: So the form exists but the data quality is the problem.
02:28 Prospect: Exactly. Garbage in. We also trialed a call-recording tool, but nobody goes back and watches recordings.
02:42 You: Is fixing this actually on your roadmap this quarter, or is it a someday thing?
02:51 Prospect: It's real. My VP put "handoff quality" as one of three Q3 OKRs. I own it.
03:04 You: Who besides you would need to sign off on bringing in a tool for this?
03:13 Prospect: I can approve up to 15k a year myself. Above that it goes to the VP of Sales, but he's the one pushing the OKR, so he's motivated.
03:30 You: If we could capture the call context automatically and push a structured handoff into Salesforce, would you be willing to run a pilot with two of your pods this month?
03:44 Prospect: If it doesn't add work for the AEs, yes. I could give you the two pods that complain the loudest.
03:58 You: Great. What would you need to see at the end of a pilot to call it a win?
04:07 Prospect: Kickoffs not slipping, and implementation rating handoff completeness above eight out of ten.
04:20 You: Let's lock it in. I'll send a pilot plan tomorrow — can we get thirty minutes Thursday with you and the implementation lead to scope it?
04:31 Prospect: Thursday works. Send the invite and I'll pull in Dana from implementation.
